Week 10 Blog - Fourth round of prototyping experiments / Planning for final presentation.

Published by 23 May 2026

Experience

After receiving feedback from Crit Session 2, I revisited the DES304 business stream brief and reflected more deeply on the overall direction of my project. During the earlier stages of the project, I focused mainly on designing a small regenerative garden space with composting, planting, and rainwater collection systems. While the feedback suggested that the overall direction was strong, I realised that my design still lacked a stronger connection to the wider goals of the brief.

The project is not only about designing for a single company or creating an isolated sustainable space. Instead, the brief focuses on climate resilience, ecological restoration, regenerative systems, and rebuilding relationships between people, businesses, communities, and the environment within the Rosedale Park area. This reflection helped me understand that my project should function more like a connected regenerative system rather than only a visual garden intervention.

Reflection on Crit Feedback

One of the most important points from the critique feedback was that the project framing had become clearer compared to earlier experiments, but the intervention still needed more detail and stronger interaction within the space. The feedback suggested that I should think more carefully about:

  • The actual scale of the intervention
  • How different spatial areas connect together
  • What kinds of plants or trees should be included
  • How businesses participate in the system
  • How people physically interact with the space
  • How the design creates long-term ecological and social value

This feedback helped me realise that I needed to move beyond creating a general “green space” and instead begin designing a more believable and site-responsive regenerative environment.

After rereading the brief, I began to understand that the project is intended to support the restoration of relationships between:

  • People and nature
  • People and local communities
  • Businesses and ecological systems
  • Modern economies and Indigenous knowledge

This shifted my thinking significantly. Rather than designing for one company, I now see the project as a system that allows businesses, volunteers, and local communities to participate together in regenerative activities such as composting, planting, food growing, biodiversity restoration, and rainwater collection.

The project therefore became less focused on creating a final object and more focused on creating a living ecological network that can evolve over time.

For the next stage of development, I plan to focus more carefully on spatial planning and ecological systems within the intervention. Instead of only improving the visual quality of the Blender model, I want to begin testing:

  • Planting layouts and biodiversity
  • Spatial zoning
  • Pathways and circulation
  • Rainwater systems
  • Composting areas
  • Edible planting systems
  • Relationships between trees, seating, and gathering spaces
  • Scale and distance between objects

I also want to research regenerative planting methods and forest garden systems to better understand how layered ecosystems can function within the intervention space.

Moving Toward Final Concepts

This reflection also helped me understand that Semester 1 is not about producing a single final outcome. Instead, it is about testing multiple concept directions before refining the strongest idea later in the project.

My next step will therefore be developing several possible regenerative intervention concepts that explore different relationships between ecology, business participation, and community interaction within Rosedale Park.
